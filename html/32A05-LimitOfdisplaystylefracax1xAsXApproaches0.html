limit of ax-1x as x approaches 0


Corollary.

For a>0, we have

limx→0⁡ax-1x=ln⁡a.
Proof.

Recall that ax=ex⁢ln⁡a. Thus,

limx→0⁡ax-1x =limx→0⁡ex⁢ln⁡a-1x
=limx→0⁡(ex⁢ln⁡a-1)⁢ln⁡ax⁢ln⁡a
=(ln⁡a)⁢limx→0⁡ex⁢ln⁡a-1x⁢ln⁡a.

Let t=x⁢ln⁡a. Then t→0 as x→0. Therefore,

limx→0⁡ax-1x =(ln⁡a)⁢limt→0⁡et-1t
=(ln⁡a)⁢1
=ln⁡a.∎

The formula from the corollary is useful for proving that dd⁢x⁢ax=ax⁢ln⁡a. On the other hand, once this fact is known, the corollary is easily proven via l’Hôpital’s rule (http://planetmath.org/LHpitalsRule):

limx→0⁡ax-1x =limx→0⁡ax⁢ln⁡a1
=a0⁢ln⁡a
=ln⁡a.
