Lewy hypersurface


The real hypersurface in (z1,…,zn)∈ℂn given by

Im⁡zn=∑j=1n-1|zj|2

is called the Lewy hypersurface. Note that this is a real hypersurface of real dimension 2⁢n-1. This is an example of a non-trivial real hypersurface in complex space. For example it is not biholomorphically equivalent to the hyperplane defined by Im⁡zn=0, but it is locally (not globally) biholomorphically equivalent to a unit sphere.
